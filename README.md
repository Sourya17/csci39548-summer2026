## Use of AI

AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.) are **allowed and encouraged** on all
assignments and the final project — *except* the proctored exit assessment.
The expectation is **informed use**: you must be able to **explain every line
you submit** in your own words.

## Getting help

You learn the most by **working through problems yourself first**. Try this order
before reaching out to me:

1. **Read the docs** — MDN, react.dev, official library docs. These are written
   by the people who built the tools and are almost always faster than asking.
2. **Search the internet** — Stack Overflow, GitHub Issues, blog posts. Almost
   every error message you'll see has been hit and answered by someone else.
3. **Use the Brightspace discussion forum** — post your question there so your
   classmates see it. **Answering other students' questions is one of the best
   ways to learn**, and the forum makes the whole class smarter together.
4. **Discuss with peers** — your final project teammates and classmates are your
   first line of debugging.
5. **Bring questions to class** — we have time at the end of every session.
6. **Email me** to set up office hours (no standing office hours by default).
   For Git/GitHub issues with submissions, contact me **before** the deadline.
